01 · Definition & Lineage

What is a world model?

A learned internal model that predicts how an environment evolves — given the current state (and optionally an action) it predicts future states, observations, and/or rewards — for planning, control, imagination, or simulation.

1943
Craik
A "small-scale model" of reality in the mind.
1990
Schmidhuber
Recurrent model net → makes the world differentiable.
1991
Sutton · Dyna
Plan by "trying things in your head".
2018
Ha & Schmidhuber
V–M–C; train a policy inside a dream.
2022
LeCun · JEPA
Predict in representation space, not pixels.
2024+
Neural engines
Interactive generative video (GameNGen, Genie).

Terminology by community: "the model" (model-based RL) · "world model" (Dreamer) · "JEPA" (LeCun) · "neural game engine / world simulator" (generative video).

02 · The Landscape

One term, three research lineages

Model-based RL

Goal: compact dynamics model to plan / learn a policy.

Space: low-dim latent · Scale: tiny (≤200M, single GPU).

DreamerMuZeroIRISTD-MPC2

Generative simulation

Goal: action-conditioned interactive video — a playable neural engine.

Space: pixels / image tokens · Scale: large (0.5–14B, clusters).

GameNGenGenieDIAMONDCosmos

Representation models

Goal: predict future representations — no pixel reconstruction.

Space: abstract latent · Scale: efficient (no decoder).

I-JEPAV-JEPA 2DINO-WM

They differ on what is predicted (reward/value vs. pixels vs. embeddings) and what it is for (control vs. simulation vs. representation).

Dreamer (RSSM) & IRIS predict latents trained with a pixel decoder (loss = recon + KL); JEPA / DINO-WM predict latents with no decoder, no reconstruction. 04 · Prediction Patterns & Objectives

Five objective families — the key design choice

Reconstruction (VAE/ELBO)PlaNet · Dreamer V1–V3
reconstruction + KL — learns a latent by regenerating observations (a reconstruction-anchored latent).
Latent self-predictionJEPA · V-JEPA · DINO-WM
‖predict − sg(target)‖ in latent space — no decoder, no pixel loss (the "representation" route); needs collapse prevention (EMA / VICReg).
Autoregressive token NLLIRIS · TWM · Genie · iVideoGPT
VQ-tokenize frames, then −log p(next token | past, action). Dynamics = sequence modeling.
Diffusion / denoisingDIAMOND · GameNGen · Oasis · Cosmos
Denoise the next frame/latent conditioned on past frames + action. High visual fidelity.
Value-equivalentMuZero · EfficientZero · TD-MPC2
Predict no pixels — only reward + value + policy (MuZero) or latent consistency + reward + value (TD-MPC2).

05 · Inputs & Outputs

What goes in, what comes out

Inputs

  • Observations — pixels (64×64 → 720p), proprioception, depth, multi-camera, text.
  • Actions / control — given discrete/continuous (Dreamer, MuZero); latent / inferred, no labels (Genie); keyboard+mouse (GameNGen, Oasis); text "world events" (Genie-3).
  • Context — a window of past frames/latents (e.g. GameNGen: last 64 frames + 64 actions).

Outputs (= the prediction target)

  • Next observation — RGB frames at some resolution & FPS (generative engines).
  • Next latent state — compact code for imagination + policy (Dreamer, IRIS).
  • Reward / value / policy — value-equivalent, no pixels (MuZero, TD-MPC2).
  • Future representation — an embedding to plan against (JEPA, DINO-WM).

Rule of thumb: the cheaper the output (reward/value < latent < representation < pixels), the cheaper the model — but the less directly interactive the rollout.

09 · Inference Cost & Efficiency Levers

Inference regimes → efficiency toolbox

Amortized policy

Cheapest. Act in one forward pass; model used only in training.

DreamerIRIS

Search / planning

Heavy per step, even if cheap to train.

MuZero MCTSTD-MPC2 MPPI

Generative rollout

Per-frame diffusion/AR; real-time only with few steps.

GameNGen 20fpsDIAMOND 10fps

Key insight: cheap to train ≠ cheap to run — a value-equivalent model (MuZero) is small but does expensive tree search at every step.

Latent-space dynamics (RSSM) — Dreamer · tokens + small transformer — Δ-IRIS (4 tok/frame).

No reconstruction (drop decoder) — JEPA, DINO-WM · self-sup consistency — EfficientZero.

Imagination + replay for sample efficiency — Atari100k regime.

Distillation / synthetic data · SSM (Mamba) backbones — long context, near-linear cost.

10 · Takeaways & References

Takeaways

  • One term, three communities — RL, generative simulation, and representation models predict different things.
  • Objective = the key design choice — recon · latent-prediction · AR-token · diffusion · value-equivalent. ("Latent state" vs "representation" is an objective distinction, not a separate space.)
  • Cost spans ~6 orders of magnitude in compute, ~4 in params; and cheap-to-train ≠ cheap-to-run.
  • Meaningful capability is already academic-scale — superhuman Atari100k, Minecraft diamond, real-robot planning, all on single GPUs.
  • Open question: which lineage & benchmark to target, and which efficiency lever (no-decoder JEPA? tokenized transformer? SSM?) to push.
