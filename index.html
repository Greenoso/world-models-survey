01 · Definition & Lineage

What is a world model?

A learned internal model that predicts how an environment evolves — given the current state (and optionally an action), it predicts future states, observations, and/or rewards — used for planning, control, imagination, or simulation.

1943
Craik
Mind carries a "small-scale model" of reality to anticipate events.
1990
Schmidhuber
Recurrent "model network" predicts future sensations → makes the world differentiable.
1991
Sutton · Dyna
Plan by "trying things in your head" using a learned model.
2018
Ha & Schmidhuber
V–M–C (VAE + MDN-RNN + controller); train a policy inside a dream.
2022
LeCun · JEPA
Predict in latent/representation space, not pixels; WM as a cognitive module.
2024+
Neural engines
Interactive generative video as a "world model" (GameNGen, Genie).

Terminology varies by community: "the model" (model-based RL) · "world model" (Ha/Schmidhuber, Dreamer) · "JEPA / world model" (LeCun) · "neural game engine / interactive generative video / world simulator" (GameNGen, Genie, Sora).

02 · The Landscape

One term, three research lineages

Model-based RL

Goal: learn a compact dynamics model to plan / learn a policy.

Space: low-dim latent state.
Scale: tiny (≤200M, single GPU).

PlaNetDreamerMuZeroIRISTD-MPC2

Generative simulation

Goal: generate action-conditioned, interactive video — a playable neural engine.

Space: pixels / image tokens.
Scale: large (0.5–14B, clusters).

GameNGenGenieDIAMONDSoraCosmos

Representation models

Goal: predict future representations — no pixel reconstruction.

Space: abstract latent.
Scale: efficient (no decoder).

I-JEPAV-JEPA 2DINO-WM

These lineages differ on what is predicted (reward/value vs. pixels vs. embeddings) and what it is for (control vs. simulation vs. representation) — the two primary taxonomy axes.

04 · Prediction Patterns & Objective Functions

Five objective families

Reconstruction (VAE/ELBO)PlaNet · Dreamer V1–V3
Maximize a variational bound: reconstruction + KL (DreamerV3 adds symlog, KL balancing, free bits). Learns latents by regenerating observations.
Latent self-predictionJEPA · V-JEPA · DINO-WM
Predict the embedding of the target from context: ‖pred − sg(target)‖ in latent space. No decoder, no pixel loss → needs collapse prevention (EMA target / VICReg).
Autoregressive token NLLIRIS · TWM · Genie · iVideoGPT
VQ-tokenize frames, then a Transformer maximizes −log p(next token | past, action). Dynamics learning becomes sequence modeling.
Diffusion / denoisingDIAMOND · GameNGen · Oasis · Cosmos
Train a denoiser to reverse a noising process and produce the next frame/latent conditioned on past frames + action. High visual fidelity.
Value-equivalentMuZero · EfficientZero · TD-MPC2
Predict no observations — only reward + value + policy (or latent consistency). The model must only be accurate for planning.

05 · Inputs & Outputs

What goes in, what comes out

Inputs

  • Observations — pixels (64×64 → 720p), proprioception, depth, multi-camera, or text.
  • Actions / control — given discrete or continuous (Dreamer, MuZero); latent / inferred with no labels (Genie); keyboard+mouse (GameNGen, Oasis); text "world events" (Genie-3).
  • Context — a window of past frames/latents (e.g. GameNGen: last 64 frames + 64 actions).

Outputs (= the prediction target)

  • Next observation — RGB frames at a resolution & FPS (generative engines).
  • Next latent state — compact code for imagination & policy learning (Dreamer, IRIS).
  • Reward / value / policy — value-equivalent models, no pixels (MuZero, TD-MPC2).
  • Future representation — an embedding to plan against (JEPA, DINO-WM).

Rule of thumb: the cheaper the output (reward/value < latent < representation < pixels), the cheaper the model — but the less directly interpretable / interactive the rollout.

08 · Notable Models III — Representation (JEPA) Models

Predict representations, not pixels

The idea

Predict the embedding of a future/masked target from context. No decoder, no reconstruction loss — the model never regenerates imperceptible pixel detail, lowering compute and ignoring unpredictable noise.

Requires collapse prevention: EMA target encoder + stop-gradient, or variance/covariance regularization (VICReg).

Closest fit to the "tiny world model" thesis: capability without a generative pixel decoder.

Milestones

  • I-JEPA (2023) — masked-image latent prediction; ViT-H trained on 16×A100, <72h.
  • V-JEPA (2024) — feature prediction on ~2M videos; 81.9% Kinetics-400.
  • V-JEPA 2 (2025) — 1.2B video WM; action-conditioned variant post-trained on <62h robot video → zero-shot pick-and-place planning.
  • DINO-WM (2024) — world model in frozen DINOv2 latent space; task-agnostic zero-shot planning via latent rollouts.

09 · Evaluation

How are world models measured?

Control / RL benchmarks

  • Atari 100k — 26 games, 100k steps (~2h play). The canonical sample-efficiency / academic-scale yardstick.
  • DM Control Suite — continuous control from state or pixels (0–1000/task).
  • Crafter — 2D survival; 22 achievements measure broad capability cheaply.
  • Minecraft (diamond) — open-ended, long-horizon, sparse reward.
  • Procgen — 16 procedural games; isolates generalization (train→test gap).

Generative-video metrics

  • Visual fidelity — FID (per frame); FVD (Fréchet Video Distance) over I3D spatiotemporal features — captures quality and dynamics; lower is better.
  • Action-controllability — does the next frame respond correctly to the input action?
  • Long-horizon consistency — does the rollout stay coherent over many seconds without drift?

No single metric is agreed upon for "is this a good world model" — a recognized open problem.

11 · Inference Cost

Three inference regimes

Amortized policy

Cheapest. A trained policy acts in one forward pass; the world model is only used during training (imagination).

DreamerIRISTWM

→ real-time control, negligible per-step cost.

Search / planning

Heavy at inference. Roll the model out many times per action — even if training is cheap.

MuZero MCTS 50–800TD-MPC2 MPPI 512PlaNet CEM 1000×10

→ latency scales with #simulations.

Generative rollout

Per-frame generation cost. Diffusion/AR steps per frame; real-time only with few steps / distillation.

GameNGen 20 FPSOasis 20 FPSDIAMOND 10 FPSGenie-3 24 FPS

→ Cosmos / GAIA / Sora are batch, not interactive.

Key insight: cheap to train ≠ cheap to run. A value-equivalent model (MuZero) is small but does expensive tree search at every step.

12 · Efficiency Levers

What makes a world model tiny?

Latent-space dynamics

Model dynamics in a compact recurrent state; predict pixels only for the loss. RSSM / Dreamer

Discrete tokens + small transformer

VQ a frame into a few tokens → tiny sequence model. IRIS · Δ-IRIS (4 tok/frame)

No reconstruction

Predict embeddings, drop the decoder & pixel loss. JEPA · DINO-WM

Imagination + replay

Train the policy on synthetic rollouts → few real interactions. Atari100k regime

Self-supervised / consistency loss

Extract more signal per sample. EfficientZero

Distillation · synthetic data · curriculum

Compress a big model into a fast one; generate data. Cosmos-Nano · GameNGen distill

Architectural angle: state-space models (S4 / Mamba) give long-context rollouts at near-linear cost — an open, under-explored lever for tiny WMs.

13 · Synthesis

Where the academic-scale frontier sits

Tiny & capable — single GPU

≤ 200M params

DreamerV3, IRIS, Δ-IRIS, DIAMOND-Atari, TD-MPC2, DINO-WM, V-JEPA 2. Reach superhuman Atari100k, solve Minecraft diamond, plan on real robots — on 1–8 GPUs.

Industrial — clusters

9–14B params

Genie, GAIA, Cosmos, Sora. Photorealistic, open-domain, interactive — but 10⁴–10⁷ GPU-hours and proprietary video corpora; many specs undisclosed.

  • Capability is not monopolized by scale: the strongest sample-efficiency results come from sub-200M, single-GPU models.
  • The gap is fidelity & open-domain generality — exactly what costs orders of magnitude more.
  • Promising tiny levers: no-reconstruction (JEPA), context-aware tokenization (Δ-IRIS), SSM backbones, distillation from large neural engines.

14 · Takeaways & References

Takeaways

  • One term, three communities — model-based RL, generative simulation, and representation models all say "world model" but predict different things.
  • Objective = the key design choice — recon · latent-prediction · AR-token · diffusion · value-equivalent, in roughly increasing pixel-fidelity and cost.
  • Cost spans ~6 orders of magnitude in compute and ~4 in parameters; and cheap-to-train ≠ cheap-to-run (search planners).
  • Meaningful capability is already academic-scale — Atari100k, DMC, Crafter, even Minecraft, on single GPUs.
  • Open question for this project: which lineage & benchmark to target, and which efficiency lever (no-decoder JEPA? tokenized transformer? SSM?) to push.
