World Models

A simple introduction

What they are · why they matter · Fei-Fei Li's view · today's main types

The big idea: imagination

You already have a world model in your head.

If you push a glass off a table, you know it will fall and break — you do not need to try it. You imagined the result.

A world model gives an AI the same ability: imagine what happens next.

What a world model does

Given where things are now, and what you do, it predicts what happens next.

Nowthe current situation
+
Actionwhat you do
→
Nextwhat the model predicts

Repeat this again and again, and the AI can "play out" the future in its head — one step at a time.

Why is this useful?

1. Plan

Try several futures in your head, then pick the best action.

2. Practice safely

Learn inside the imagination instead of the real world — cheaper and safer (no broken robots).

3. Understand

To predict the world well, the model must learn how the world works.

This is why world models are seen as a path toward AI that truly understands the physical world.

Fei-Fei Li: beyond language

Today's chatbots are great with words, but they have never touched the world.

Today's AI models are "eloquent but inexperienced, knowledgeable but ungrounded." — Fei-Fei Li, From Words to Worlds: Spatial Intelligence is AI's Next Frontier (Nov 2025)

Her answer is spatial intelligence: understanding the 3D physical world — the capability that links imagination, perception, and action. She calls it "AI's next frontier."

Fei-Fei Li's definition of a world model

A world model is a new type of generative model that can understand, reason about, generate, and interact with complex worlds — worlds that are rich in meaning, physics, geometry, and motion. — Fei-Fei Li (2025), paraphrased from her essay

She says a world model needs three things:

Generative

It can create worlds that look and behave consistently.

Multimodal

It works with images, video, text, actions — not just one input.

Interactive

Give it an action, it returns the next state.

Spatial world models in practice

Fei-Fei Li co-founded World Labs (2024). Their model Marble (2025) builds explorable 3D worlds from a sentence, a picture, or a video — and you can walk through them and export them.

"World models should reconstruct, generate, and simulate 3D worlds, and let humans and agents interact with them." — World Labs

How "spatial" differs from the others:

  • RL world models (Dreamer): a hidden "game sense" that helps an AI player plan.
  • 2D video models (Sora, Genie): predict the next video frame — can drift over time.
  • Spatial models (World Labs): build a real 3D place you can move around in, that stays consistent.

The first four predict 2D pictures or codes. The last one (Fei-Fei Li's direction) predicts a 3D world.

Remember three things

  • A world model predicts what happens next — it is imagination for AI.
  • It is useful to plan, to practice safely, and to understand the world.
  • Fei-Fei Li's spatial intelligence pushes world models from 2D video to real, explorable 3D worlds — what she calls "AI's next frontier".
